§ 1 · Identification of the Text

The uploaded text presents approximately 1,000 lines of dense, fragmented prose. Language assignment: The text belongs to the German family (West Germanic, Indo-European) and most closely resembles a corrupted form of Early New High German (c. 1350–1650 CE), with some features of Middle High German. § 3 · Phrase Structure Grammar (C-Structure Rules)

In Lexical-Functional Grammar, the c-structure (constituent structure) encodes surface phrase geometry before functional annotation. The following context-free rules are extracted from recurring patterns.

CP → C' C' → C IP IP → NP I' I' → I VP VP → (NP) (PP) (AdvP) V' V' → V (NP) (PP) (CP) NP → (Det) (AdjP) N' N' → N (PP) (CP) Det → der | die | das | den | dem | des | ein | eine | einen | einem | eines AdjP→ (AdvP) Adj PP → P NP AdvP → Adv (AdvP) — VERB-SECOND (V2) RULE (Germanic) — IP → XP[TOP] I[+fin] NP[SUBJ] VP ← main clause IP → NP[SUBJ] I[+fin] VP ← when SUBJ is topic IP → NP[SUBJ] VP I[+fin] ← embedded / SOV
Key Germanic feature: This language is V2 in main clauses, SOV in embedded clauses — the defining word-order typology of German. The corrupted text preserves both orders: finite verbs appear second in main clauses (ich…war, wir…weren) and clause-final in subordinate contexts (daß…war, den wan wir weren).
